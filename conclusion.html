Conclusion

Activity Theory

Activity theory has the largest scope of the three theories. It examines the relationship between a subject, an object, and mediators. What makes Activity Theory unique is that it can reveal how each component in an activity shapes other components and develops over time.

Coordination Theory

In a medium scope, coordination theory concentrates on the specific components and the interdependencies in collaboration, breaking down the process in which people work together.

Social Translucency

Having the smallest scope, social translucency theory illustrates how translucency in a social context affect users in a collaborative online system.
